Below Replacement, But Not Beyond Risk: The
Silent Crisis of Child Mortality in Odisha

Child health is one of the most important aspects for understanding the current health conditions and future health status of the population in the state. The important indicators to understand this aspect include Infant Mortality Rate (IMR), Under-five Mortality Rate (U5MR), nutritional status, causes of death, and prevalence of chronic diseases in children, among others.
Despite achieving replacement-level fertility since 2013 (SRS- 2014) and a declining trend in child mortality, Odisha still has relatively high infant mortality rates, implying more severe conditions than underdeveloped regions with relatively high fertility and high mortality rates.

Source: NFHS-4,5

Odisha is facing a unique population challenge: while families are choosing to have fewer children (a declining birth rate), unfortunately, too many babies and young children are still dying before their fifth birthday.
Normally, having fewer children is a sign of progress, leading to slower population growth and potentially a stronger workforce in the future. However, when child deaths remain high, it means that even though there are fewer births, the children who are born aren't surviving at the rate they should. This points to serious issues in our healthcare system, particularly regarding the quality of care for mothers and children, and access to good nutrition, especially in villages and tribal areas.
This situation creates a tough problem for Odisha's future. If fewer children are being born and a significant number of them don't survive to adulthood, our future workforce could shrink. This might slow down economic growth. On a personal level, families face immense emotional and financial stress from losing children. To truly benefit from having smaller families and to build a strong future, Odisha must urgently improve child health services and reduce child mortality. This will ensure that every child born has the chance to thrive, contributing to a healthier and more prosperous state.
