Population Ageing

The 21st century marks a global demographic transformation, shifting from a predominantly young population to one increasingly defined by older age groups. Today, over 727 million people worldwide are aged 65 and above, a trend known as population ageing. While initially prominent in developed nations, ageing is now rapidly reshaping societies in developing countries, including India.
India’s elderly population is projected to double from 10.5% in 2022 to 20.8% by 2050. Odisha mirrors this trend, with ageing accelerating due to a below-replacement fertility rate of 1.8 and a declining youth population. Odisha’s experience aligns more with southern states like Kerala and Telangana, making it a significant case for understanding the implications of ageing in eastern India.
This section explores the causes, patterns, and consequences of population ageing in Odisha. It assesses the demographic shift’s impact on health, economy, infrastructure, social security, and governance, and offers policy insights to address emerging challenges while tapping into the potential of the elderly as contributors to development.
